FORD F-150 2021 OWNER'S QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE
(Excerpted for Digital Assistant Use)
===============================================

SECTION 3: SCHEDULED MAINTENANCE INTERVALS
-------------------------------------------

Ford recommends following the Intelligent Oil-Life Monitor (IOLM) system
built into your vehicle. The following intervals are conservative guidelines
for drivers who prefer fixed-interval scheduling:

3.1 Engine Oil & Filter
  Recommended interval: Every 5,000 miles OR every 6 months, whichever comes first.
  Oil specification: Motorcraft SAE 5W-30 Full Synthetic Motor Oil (or equivalent
    meeting Ford specification WSS-M2C946-B1).
  Note: Using full synthetic oil is required to qualify for Ford's powertrain
    warranty coverage on 2021+ EcoBoost engines.
  Reset procedure: After oil change, hold the OK button on the steering wheel
    for 3 seconds to reset the oil life indicator.

3.2 Tire Rotation
  Recommended interval: Every 7,500 miles OR every 6 months, whichever comes first.
  Rotation pattern: Front-to-rear for non-directional tires (standard on XLT).
  Note: Skipping or delaying tire rotation voids the tire manufacturer's treadwear
    warranty on most brands.

3.3 Brake System
  Recommended inspection interval: Every 15,000 miles or annually.
  Brake fluid replacement: Every 2 years regardless of mileage.
  Warning signs requiring immediate attention: grinding noise, vibration when
    braking, increased stopping distance, brake warning light.

3.4 Air Filter (Engine)
  Recommended replacement interval: Every 30,000 miles under normal conditions.
  Every 15,000 miles if driving frequently on unpaved roads.

3.5 Cabin Air Filter
  Recommended replacement interval: Every 15,000-25,000 miles or annually.
  Access: Behind the glove box (see Section 7.2 for replacement instructions).

3.6 Spark Plugs
  Recommended replacement interval: Every 60,000 miles (platinum plugs).
  Using non-OEM or copper plugs is not recommended for the 3.5L EcoBoost engine.

3.7 Transmission Fluid (10-Speed Automatic)
  Recommended change interval: Every 75,000 miles under normal driving conditions.
  Every 37,500 miles if towing frequently (more than 5,000 lbs).

3.8 Cooling System / Antifreeze
  Recommended flush interval: Every 100,000 miles or 10 years.

SECTION 4: TIRE INFORMATION
----------------------------

4.1 Factory Tire Specifications (2021 F-150 XLT)
  Tire size: P275/65R18
  Recommended tire pressure: 35 PSI (front), 35 PSI (rear) — standard load
  Maximum load: See door placard on driver side door jamb
  Spare tire: Full-size steel spare (P275/65R18) — located under truck bed

4.2 Tire Warning Light
  The TPMS warning light will illuminate when tire pressure drops 25% below
  recommended PSI. Check all four tires plus spare.

SECTION 5: VEHICLE REGISTRATION AND LEGAL REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Registration Renewal (Texas)
  Texas law requires annual vehicle registration renewal.
  Renewal notices are mailed approximately 90 days before expiry.
  Online renewal available at TxDMV.gov.
  Note: Vehicle must pass annual safety and emissions inspection BEFORE
  registration can be renewed in Travis County (Austin).

5.2 Inspection Requirements
  Texas requires a combined safety + emissions inspection annually.
  Inspection must be completed before registration renewal.
  Approved inspection stations can be found at DPS.Texas.gov.

SECTION 6: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
---------------------------------

6.1 Roadside Assistance
  Ford Roadside Assistance: 1-800-241-3673
  Available 24/7 for the first 5 years or 60,000 miles of ownership.
  Coverage includes: towing, flat tire, fuel delivery, battery jump, lockout.
  Note: Ford Roadside Assistance is separate from insurance roadside coverage.
  Check your insurance card for additional roadside assistance benefits.

6.2 Flat Tire Procedure
  1. Pull off road safely, turn on hazard lights.
  2. Apply parking brake.
  3. Remove spare from undercarriage (jack and tools in rear cargo area, left side).
  4. Loosen lug nuts BEFORE jacking vehicle.
  5. Jack vehicle at frame rail (see diagram in full manual Section 6.2).
  6. Remove flat, mount spare, hand-tighten lug nuts.
  7. Lower vehicle, torque lug nuts to 150 ft-lbs in star pattern.
  8. Do not exceed 65 mph on spare tire.
